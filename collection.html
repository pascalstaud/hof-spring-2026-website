Spring Summer 2026

PRELUDE.

Born of a Moment.

Prelude is not a season.

It's a shift. Light returns. Colours soften. Structure gives way to movement. Something held in tension begins to open.

HOF composed this moment into an object. Not the bloom — the state before it. A composition that carries that tension in its surfaces, its materials, its silence.

Parisian couture culture meets botanical precision. The Collector Model of Spring Summer 2026.

THE
MOMENT
BEFORE.

In couture, the idea arrives before the object. The narrative precedes the garment. The collection is presented before a single piece is cut.

Prelude started with a reference: early botanical illustration. The precision of line. The patience of observation. A draughtsman in 18th-century Paris spending weeks on a single stem — not to own it, but to understand it.

From there: a tonal range between champagne and indigo. Satin finishes that catch light like a petal catches dew. Botanical textile inserts woven exclusively for this composition. A silhouette interpreted, never altered.

HOF G · V — Prelude · Collector Model · SS26

Shaped by Couture.

Every surface is a deliberate act. Two tones — warm champagne and a deeper ground — in a satin finish that holds light, softens it, lets it go. Where the industry pursues gloss, HOF pursues absorption.

Inside, the atmosphere shifts. Textile inserts carry a botanical jacquard created for Prelude alone — a floral language drawn from the Kenzo couture tradition, reinterpreted in grey and ivory. Warm leather, vegetable-tanned, chosen for tactility. Embroidery visible only up close — a private language for those who look.

The cabin is a room. Composed, not configured.

Exterior — Satin finish, two-tone composition, discreet HOF identification.

Interior — Botanical textile inserts, hand-placed embroidery, warm leather palette.

Detail — Every stitch placed by hand at Atelier HOF, Sindelfingen.

A Composition You Inhabit.

Presence. Not the kind that announces — the kind you notice once you're inside. A mood. A material. A silence. Something composed with the same attention you bring to the things that matter to you.

This is what HOF curates. Not specification. Identity.

"True elegance appears at the moment something opens, not when it insists."

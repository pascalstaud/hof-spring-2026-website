Essence Model · Spring Summer 2026

Ma.

The luxury of nothing. Where negative space becomes the composition.

The Question

What remains when you remove
everything that doesn't belong?

The Japanese concept of Ma — the interval, the pause, the space between — is the thing that gives everything else meaning.

Ma began not with addition but with subtraction. A Salon Silhouette, cleaned of ornament. Standard doors, not coach doors. A single exterior color — all silver, a singular composition. The discipline of knowing what to leave out.

"The most expensive thing in Tokyo real estate is empty space."

Silver meets sand — the dialogue between cold surface and warm textile.

The Answer

Two materials.
One quiet rebellion.

Two materials define Ma's interior world. Bouclé — a high-pile woven textile from the world of luxury furniture. Warm, tactile, inviting. It covers seats, dashboard surfaces, door panels. You sit inside Ma and feel at home.

Metallic leather — leather finished to look like brushed aluminum. Cold appearance, warm reality. When you touch the dashboard, you expect metal. Instead, you find warmth. This surprise is Ma's quiet rebellion.

Sand, cream, olive. The palette of a living room you never want to leave. A living room on wheels.

The transition — where metallic leather meets bouclé, cold appearance meets warm reality.

HOF G · V — Ma · All Silver · The beauty of what remains when everything unnecessary is removed.

The cabin — bouclé textile, metallic leather, H-pattern embossed into every surface.

The exterior — all silver, cleaned Salon Silhouette. Nothing added. Nothing missing.

The cockpit — bouclé wraps every surface. A living room, not a machine.

The Ma Lounge Chair — bouclé from car to living room. The same textile, the same warmth.

The rear — H badge, minimal taillights. Every detail earned its place.

The front — H-pattern grille, the only ornament on a surface defined by absence.

One composition.
No options.

Silver exterior. Sand-cream-olive interior. No configurator. No choices. One configuration. One answer. The rarest luxury: a maker confident enough to say this is it.

The H-pattern appears in decorative trim elements — the only geometric gesture in an otherwise fluid world. Everything else is subtraction: clean surfaces, quiet transitions, the beauty of what is not there.

"The rarest luxury: a maker confident enough to say this is it."

The weave — where patience becomes texture, hand becomes home.

Wabi-sabi — the beauty found in imperfection, the calm in restraint.

The chair, at home — bouclé in its natural habitat.

Architecture meets automotive — the same design language, different scales.

The Throw — bouclé warmth, carried from cabin to couch.

The Cushion Pair — sand and olive, the Ma palette in miniature.

A moment. Hands, texture, stillness.

The world around the car — ceramic, textile, nature.

Epilogue

Ma.

The luxury of nothing. The space between things, where meaning lives.

Not a car with less. A car with only what matters. The interval. The pause. The silence that makes the music.

"Curated, Not Made."
